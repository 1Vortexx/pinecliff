1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 PURPOSE OF THIS HANDBOOK

Welcome to the Pinecliff County, CO Roleplay Staff Handbook. This comprehensive

document serves as the definitive guide for all staff members, outlining

expectations, procedures, and best practices for maintaining our community's

standards of excellence.

This handbook exists to:

  • Establish clear and consistent standards for all staff members
  • Provide detailed procedures for common and uncommon situations
  • Minimize confusion and gray areas in staff decision-making
  • Protect both staff members and community members through transparent policies
  • Create a framework for fair and equitable enforcement of community rules
  • Support staff development and professional growth within the community

1.2 SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

This handbook applies to ALL staff members, including but not limited to:

  • Server Hosts
  • In-Game Moderators
  • Discord Moderators
  • Discord Administrators
  • High Command Members
  • Management Team Members
  • Trial Staff and Interns
  • Any individual with elevated permissions or responsibilities

All staff members are expected to be familiar with and adhere to every section

of this handbook relevant to their role. Ignorance of handbook policies is not

an acceptable defense for policy violations.

1.3 STAFF MEMBER ACKNOWLEDGMENT

Upon joining the staff team or upon any major handbook revision, all staff

members must:

  • Read the entire handbook thoroughly
  • Acknowledge receipt and understanding of the handbook
  • Sign or digitally confirm acceptance of handbook terms
  • Ask questions about any unclear policies before beginning duties

Failure to acknowledge the handbook may result in temporary suspension of staff

privileges until acknowledgment is completed.

1.4 UPDATES AND REVISIONS

This handbook is a living document and may be updated periodically to reflect:

  • Community growth and evolution
  • New features or platforms
  • Clarifications based on real situations
  • Legal or policy changes
  • Staff feedback and suggestions

Major revisions will be announced through official channels. Staff members are

responsible for staying informed about updates. The effective date at the top

of this document indicates the most recent revision.

2. GENERAL CONDUCT GUIDELINES

2.1 PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS

As a staff member of Pinecliff County, CO Roleplay, you are held to a higher

standard of conduct than regular community members. Professional behavior is

required at all times when acting in any staff capacity.

PROFESSIONALISM INCLUDES:

Courtesy and Respect

  • Treat all community members with dignity, regardless of their history
  • Respond to questions and concerns in a helpful, patient manner
  • Avoid dismissive, condescending, or sarcastic responses
  • Remain calm and composed even when dealing with difficult individuals
  • Never engage in personal attacks or insults

Reliability

  • Be present and active during scheduled shifts or commitments
  • Respond to staff mentions and urgent matters in a timely manner
  • Follow through on commitments and assigned tasks
  • Notify leadership if you will be unavailable for extended periods
  • Maintain consistent activity to retain your staff position

Competence

  • Know the rules thoroughly and apply them correctly
  • Stay updated on policy changes and community developments
  • Ask for guidance when uncertain rather than guessing
  • Continuously improve your skills and knowledge
  • Take training opportunities seriously

Impartiality

  • Make decisions based on rules and evidence, not personal feelings
  • Avoid showing favoritism toward friends or certain members
  • Recuse yourself from situations involving close friends when appropriate
  • Apply consequences consistently regardless of who is involved
  • Never use staff powers for personal gain or revenge

2.2 ROLE MODEL EXPECTATIONS

Staff members represent Pinecliff County, CO Roleplay and must exemplify the

community's values.

YOU ARE EXPECTED TO:

  • Follow ALL community rules, even minor ones
  • Maintain a positive attitude and encourage constructive interactions
  • Resolve conflicts peacefully and diplomatically
  • Demonstrate good sportsmanship in all activities
  • Show respect for fellow staff members, even in disagreements
  • Uphold community standards in your own behavior before enforcing them on others
  • Be a positive influence who improves the community atmosphere

YOU MUST NOT:

  • Engage in rule violations and claim immunity due to staff status
  • Participate in drama, gossip, or community divisions
  • Publicly complain about staff decisions or leadership
  • Undermine other staff members' authority or decisions
  • Use your position to intimidate, manipulate, or coerce others
  • Behave in ways that bring negative attention to the community

2.3 CONFIDENTIALITY AND PRIVACY

Staff members have access to sensitive information that must be protected.

CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION INCLUDES:

  • Internal Affairs investigations and findings
  • Private staff discussions and deliberations
  • Personal information about community members
  • Moderation history and case details (unless public)
  • Security measures, passwords, or access credentials
  • Planned announcements before official release
  • Private conversations in staff channels
  • Internal conflicts or disagreements among staff

CONFIDENTIALITY REQUIREMENTS:

  • Never discuss confidential matters in public channels or with non-staff
  • Do not share screenshots of staff channels or private discussions
  • Avoid naming specific individuals when discussing cases publicly
  • Use secure methods when sharing sensitive information
  • Delete sensitive information from personal devices when no longer needed
  • Report any confidentiality breaches you observe
  • Maintain confidentiality even after leaving the staff team

EXCEPTIONS TO CONFIDENTIALITY:

Confidentiality may be breached only when:

  • Legally required (law enforcement requests, court orders)
  • Necessary to prevent imminent harm to individuals
  • Explicitly authorized by High Command or Management
  • The information becomes publicly available through official channels

2.4 CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

Staff members must recognize and appropriately handle conflicts of interest.

CONFLICTS OF INTEREST OCCUR WHEN:

  • You must moderate a close friend or relative
  • You have personal involvement in a situation requiring moderation
  • You have a strong personal bias that may affect fair judgment
  • You would benefit personally from a particular decision
  • You have existing disputes or tensions with involved parties

HANDLING CONFLICTS OF INTEREST:

  1. Recognize the conflict and disclose it immediately
  2. Request another staff member to handle the situation
  3. Document the conflict and your recusal
  4. Do not attempt to influence the handling of the matter
  5. Accept the decisions made by neutral staff members

PROHIBITED ACTIONS:

  • Failing to disclose known conflicts of interest
  • Participating in decisions despite obvious conflicts
  • Using your position to settle personal scores
  • Showing favoritism to friends or associates
  • Retaliating against those who report you for conflicts of interest

2.5 COMMUNICATION STANDARDS

How staff members communicate reflects on the entire community.

LANGUAGE REQUIREMENTS:

  • Keep all language clean, professional, and non-explicit
  • Avoid excessive profanity, even in casual staff channels
  • Never use slurs, hate speech, or discriminatory language
  • Refrain from sexual, graphic, or inappropriate content
  • Use proper grammar and spelling in official communications
  • Avoid excessive use of all caps (seen as shouting)

TONE AND DEMEANOR:

  • Remain respectful and professional in all interactions
  • Avoid aggressive, hostile, or confrontational language
  • Stay patient when explaining rules or policies repeatedly
  • Keep responses helpful and solution-oriented
  • Acknowledge valid concerns and frustrations empathetically
  • De-escalate tensions rather than inflaming them

COMMUNICATION CHANNELS:

  • Use appropriate channels for different types of communication
  • Keep moderation discussions in staff channels
  • Direct urgent matters to appropriate staff members
  • Avoid excessive off-topic conversation in work channels
  • Respect channel purposes and guidelines

2.6 PROHIBITED BEHAVIORS

The following behaviors are absolutely prohibited for all staff members:

NEVER ALLOWED:

  • Abuse of staff powers or permissions
  • Harassment, bullying, or targeting of community members
  • Sharing personal information (doxxing) about anyone
  • Creating or participating in witch hunts against individuals
  • Threatening violence or harm against anyone
  • Discriminating based on race, religion, gender, sexuality, nationality, etc.
  • Soliciting or sharing illegal content
  • Impersonating other staff members or community members
  • Lying to leadership or falsifying reports
  • Intentionally misleading community members about policies
  • Using alternate accounts to evade accountability
  • Participating in or organizing raids/attacks on other communities
  • Accepting bribes or special treatment in exchange for leniency
  • Sharing confidential community information with competing communities

CONSEQUENCES:

First offense: Immediate removal from staff team and potential community ban

These behaviors will not be tolerated under any circumstances.

2.7 STAFF INTERACTIONS WITH COMMUNITY MEMBERS

BOUNDARIES AND PROFESSIONALISM:

  • Maintain appropriate boundaries with community members
  • Avoid romantic or overly personal relationships that could compromise judgment
  • Keep interactions primarily focused on community matters
  • Don't share excessive personal information
  • Redirect personal problems to appropriate resources
  • Be friendly but maintain professional distance

FAIRNESS AND IMPARTIALITY:

  • Treat all members equally regardless of personal feelings
  • Never threaten to use staff powers in personal disputes
  • Separate personal relationships from staff duties
  • Don't make promises of special treatment
  • Apply rules consistently to everyone, including friends

HANDLING COMPLAINTS:

  • Listen to complaints professionally and without defensiveness
  • Thank members for bringing concerns to your attention
  • Investigate fairly and thoroughly
  • Follow up on resolutions
  • Escalate to higher staff if needed
  • Don't dismiss complaints as "just drama"

2.8 STAFF-TO-STAFF INTERACTIONS

TEAMWORK:

  • Support fellow staff members in their duties
  • Offer assistance when colleagues are overwhelmed
  • Share knowledge and best practices
  • Celebrate successes and learn from mistakes together
  • Present a unified front to the community

DISAGREEMENTS:

  • Discuss disagreements privately, never publicly
  • Focus on issues, not personal attacks
  • Seek compromise and common ground
  • Escalate to leadership if unable to resolve
  • Accept final decisions gracefully
  • Never undermine another staff member publicly

RESPECTING HIERARCHY:

  • Understand the chain of command
  • Follow directions from higher-ranking staff
  • Escalate issues appropriately
  • Don't circumvent your immediate supervisors
  • Offer suggestions respectfully
  • Support leadership decisions even if you disagree privately
  1. IN-GAME MODERATION IN SESSIONS

3.1 STAFF RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

IN-GAME MODERATORS ARE RESPONSIBLE FOR:

Monitoring and Observation

  • Actively watch gameplay for rule violations
  • Use free cam to observe different areas of the map
  • Pay attention to chat for reports or concerning behavior
  • Notice patterns of problematic behavior
  • Stay alert throughout the entire session

Player Assistance

  • Answer questions about rules and gameplay
  • Help new players understand server mechanics
  • Provide guidance on proper roleplay etiquette
  • Resolve minor disputes between players
  • Explain consequences and warnings clearly

Rule Enforcement

  • Take appropriate action against rule violators
  • Issue warnings, kicks, and bans as necessary
  • Document all moderation actions properly
  • Maintain consistency in enforcement
  • Follow the progressive discipline system

Session Management

  • Ensure smooth gameplay for all participants
  • Address lag or technical issues when possible
  • Manage session settings appropriately
  • Coordinate with server host on major decisions
  • Handle emergency situations calmly

Communication

  • Keep players informed of expectations
  • Announce important information clearly
  • Respond to mentions and questions promptly
  • Coordinate with other staff members present
  • Report significant issues to leadership

MODERATORS MUST NOT:

  • Participate in roleplay while actively moderating
  • Ignore rule violations because they're minor
  • Over-punish minor infractions
  • Make up rules that don't exist
  • Moderate while AFK or distracted
  • Use powers to gain advantages in gameplay
  • Retaliate against players who annoy them
  • Allow friends to break rules without consequence

3.2 STAFF IDENTIFICATION AND APPEARANCE

REQUIRED UNIFORM:

When on active moderation duty, staff members MUST wear:

  • Snow Shirt (available in game)
  • Snow Pants (available in game)

This uniform makes staff members easily identifiable to players who need

assistance or have concerns.

UNIFORM REQUIREMENTS:

  • Must be worn for the entire duration of your moderation duties
  • Must be visible (don't hide in vehicles constantly)
  • Change to uniform before taking any moderation actions
  • If participating in roleplay later, change out of uniform first

EXCEPTIONS:

  • Undercover investigations authorized by High Command
  • Technical issues preventing character customization
  • Special events with approved alternative dress codes

OFF-DUTY CONDUCT:

When not wearing the staff uniform:

  • You may participate in roleplay normally
  • You are still expected to follow all community rules
  • You should still report serious violations you witness
  • You may be called to duty if situations require additional staff
  • You remain a representative of the staff team

3.3 MONITORING AND OBSERVATION PRACTICES

EFFECTIVE MONITORING TECHNIQUES:

Use Free Cam Regularly

  • Cycle through different areas of the map
  • Watch high-traffic locations more frequently
  • Check isolated areas where violations may occur
  • Observe reported individuals or situations
  • Don't stay in one spot the entire session

Watch Chat Activity

  • Monitor for arguments or conflicts
  • Look for rule violation discussions
  • Notice spam or inappropriate content
  • Pay attention to player reports
  • Track patterns of complaints about specific individuals

Observe Player Behavior

  • Watch for VDM (Vehicle Death Match)
  • Look for RDM (Random Death Match) if weapons are enabled
  • Notice players disrupting roleplay intentionally
  • Identify harassment or targeted behavior
  • Track players who consistently cause issues

PROACTIVE VS. REACTIVE MODERATION:

Proactive (Preferred)

  • Stop violations before they escalate
  • Intervene in developing conflicts
  • Warn players exhibiting concerning behavior
  • Educate players on proper conduct

Reactive (When Necessary)

  • Respond to player reports
  • Address violations after they occur
  • Take action based on evidence
  • Handle situations that weren't prevented

BALANCING PRESENCE:

  • Be visible enough to deter violations
  • Don't be so intrusive that you disrupt gameplay
  • Let minor issues resolve naturally when appropriate
  • Intervene in serious matters immediately
  • Give players space to roleplay while maintaining oversight

3.4 STRUCTURING AND CONDUCTING MOD SCENES

A "Mod Scene" is a private discussion between staff and a player regarding a

rule violation or concern.

WHEN TO INITIATE A MOD SCENE:

Required For:

  • Any rule violation requiring consequences beyond a verbal warning
  • Multiple minor violations by the same player
  • Complaints or reports about a player's behavior
  • Suspected but unconfirmed violations requiring investigation
  • Situations requiring explanation or clarification
  • Appeals of previous punishments

Not Required For:

  • Simple questions or assistance requests
  • First-time minor infractions with verbal warnings
  • General announcements or reminders
  • Routine check-ins with players

MOD SCENE PROCEDURE:

Step 1: Initiation

  • Announce in text chat: "(USERNAME) Please join MS#" (replace # with mod scene

channel number, e.g., MS1)

  • Be specific about which player you're addressing
  • Use their current display name to avoid confusion
  • Wait a reasonable time for them to join (2-3 minutes)

Step 2: Voice Channel Requirement

  • Player must join the designated mod scene voice channel
  • Ensure they can hear you and you can hear them
  • If they have technical issues, troubleshoot briefly
  • Document if they refuse to join
  • Give reasonable accommodation for legitimate technical problems

Step 3: Present Evidence

  • Explain clearly what rule violation occurred
  • Show evidence (screenshots, video clips, logs)
  • Give the player opportunity to respond
  • Listen to their explanation fully
  • Ask clarifying questions if needed

Step 4: Determine Action

  • Decide appropriate consequence based on:

* Severity of violation

* Player's history

* Intent (accidental vs. deliberate)

* Cooperation during mod scene

* Mitigating factors

  • Explain your decision clearly
  • Tell them specifically what rule was broken
  • Clarify expectations going forward

Step 5: Apply Consequence

  • Use appropriate command to issue warning, kick, or ban
  • Document the incident in your moderation notes
  • Add case notes explaining the situation
  • Answer any final questions the player has

Step 6: Follow-up

  • Monitor the player after they return (if kicked)
  • Watch for retaliation or continued violations
  • Escalate to higher staff if problems persist

FAILURE TO JOIN MOD SCENE:

If a player refuses or fails to join the mod scene voice channel after proper

notice and reasonable wait time:

Immediate Action:

  • Kick the player from the in-game server
  • Issue a warning in the Discord server
  • Document the refusal in the case notes

Reasoning:

  • Cooperation with staff is required
  • Mod scenes ensure fair process
  • Technical issues should be communicated
  • Refusal suggests guilt or non-compliance

Exceptions:

  • Player has legitimate technical issues they're trying to resolve
  • Player is temporarily AFK but returns within reasonable time (5 minutes)
  • Emergency situations in real life (must be communicated)

MOD SCENE BEST PRACTICES:

DO:

  • Remain calm and professional throughout
  • Speak clearly and avoid aggressive tones
  • Give the player chance to explain
  • Be fair and unbiased
  • Document everything important
  • Thank cooperative players for their time
  • Use mod scenes as teaching opportunities

DON'T:

  • Yell or berate players
  • Make assumptions without evidence
  • Rush through the process
  • Refuse to listen to explanations
  • Bring personal feelings into decisions
  • Discuss unrelated issues
  • Allow other players to interrupt

3.5 MODERATION TOOLS AND COMMANDS

Staff members must be thoroughly familiar with all available moderation

commands and use them appropriately.

COMMAND REFERENCE:

B!ssuvote

Purpose: Initiates a session start-up vote

When to Use: Before starting any official session to gauge player readiness

Requirements: Vote count MUST be set to 5

Notes: All eligible players should be given opportunity to vote

B!ssu

Purpose: Starts an official session

When to Use: After successful session vote

Requirements: PT (Private Type) MUST be set to "strict" unless a High Rank

Intern or higher explicitly authorizes otherwise

Notes: Verify all settings before executing

B!ssd

Purpose: Closes an official session

When to Use: At the end of session or if session must be terminated early

Requirements: Reason should be set to "concluded" for normal endings

Notes: Announce session ending to players first when possible

/warn

Purpose: Issues a formal warning to a user

When to Use:

  • Minor to moderate rule violations
  • First-time offenses (usually)
  • Educational purposes
  • Building documentation before stronger action

Parameters: Username, reason (be specific and clear)

Notes: Warnings accumulate in user's history

/note add

Purpose: Adds a note to a user's record

When to Use:

  • Session bans or temporary restrictions
  • Important information about the user
  • Context for future staff reference
  • Non-punitive documentation

Example: "Session barred from 11-21-25 to 12-15-25 for repeated VDM"

Notes: Notes don't count as punishments but provide valuable context

/user history

Purpose: Views complete moderation history for a user

When to Use:

  • Before taking action against a repeat offender
  • Investigating patterns of behavior
  • Determining appropriate consequence level
  • Reviewing past issues with a player

Notes: Consider full history, not just recent incidents

/case view

Purpose: Views details of a specific moderation case

When to Use:

  • Reviewing past incidents
  • Investigating appeals
  • Understanding context of previous actions
  • Verifying information

Parameters: Case number

Notes: Case numbers are assigned automatically

/mute add

Purpose: Times out a member (prevents them from speaking/typing)

When to Use:

  • Spam or excessive chat violations
  • Verbal harassment
  • Temporary cooling-off periods
  • Minor chat-based disruptions

Parameters: Username, duration, reason

Notes: Use shortest effective duration

COMMAND USAGE GUIDELINES:

Before Using Any Command:

  • Verify you have the correct username
  • Double-check your reason for accuracy
  • Ensure the action is appropriate for the violation
  • Consider the player's history
  • Document your reasoning

After Using Any Command:

  • Verify the command executed successfully
  • Add detailed case notes
  • Inform the player of the action taken
  • Monitor for any follow-up needed
  • Report significant cases to leadership

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Typos in usernames (wrong person punished)
  • Vague or unclear reasons
  • Wrong severity of punishment
  • Forgetting to document
  • Acting on incomplete information

3.6 HANDLING RULE VIOLATIONS

Rule violations should be handled systematically and fairly.

VIOLATION ASSESSMENT:

When a potential violation occurs, assess:

Severity

  • Minor: Small infractions, easily corrected
  • Moderate: Clear violations, disrupting but not severe
  • Major: Serious violations, harmful to community
  • Critical: Dangerous, illegal, or severely harmful behavior

Intent

  • Accidental: Genuine mistakes, lack of knowledge
  • Reckless: Carelessness without malicious intent
  • Deliberate: Intentional rule-breaking
  • Malicious: Intended to cause harm or disruption

Context

  • Was the player provoked?
  • Were there mitigating circumstances?
  • Is this part of a pattern?
  • Did the player attempt to stop or correct the behavior?

History

  • First-time offense?
  • Repeat offender?
  • Recent violations or clean record?
  • Previous warnings or punishments?

Impact

  • How many people were affected?
  • Was roleplay significantly disrupted?
  • Did anyone suffer harassment or harm?
  • Can the situation be easily corrected?

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:

Based on your assessment, choose the appropriate response:

Verbal Warning (Informal)

When: First-time minor infractions, accidental violations

How: Explain the rule, ask them to stop, monitor for compliance

Document: Optional, but note if part of pattern

Formal Warning (/warn)

When: Repeated minor violations, first-time moderate violations

How: Issue warning command with specific reason

Document: Required, always document formal warnings

Kick from Server

When: Moderate violations, failure to comply with warnings, pattern of issues

How: Kick command with explanation, allow return after cooling off

Document: Required, detailed notes on circumstances

Temporary Ban

When: Major violations, severe disruptions, repeated moderate violations

How: Ban command with specified duration and clear reason

Document: Required, comprehensive documentation

Permanent Ban

When: Critical violations, repeated major violations, no reform shown

How: Ban command with no expiration, detailed justification

Document: Required, extensive documentation, leadership notification

SPECIFIC VIOLATION TYPES:

VDM (Vehicle Death Match - Intentional Vehicle Ramming)

First Offense: Formal warning

Second Offense: Kick from session

Third Offense: 1-3 day session ban

Severe/Mass VDM: Immediate kick or longer ban

RDM (Random Death Match - Unjustified Killing)

First Offense: Formal warning

Second Offense: Kick from session

Third Offense: 3-7 day session ban

Mass RDM: Immediate ban, 7-30 days

FailRP (Unrealistic Roleplay)

First Offense: Verbal or formal warning

Repeated: Kick from session

Persistent: Session ban

Harassment

First Offense: Formal warning (minimum)

Continued: Immediate kick

Severe: Ban, 7-30 days or permanent

Discriminatory Behavior/Hate Speech

First Offense: Immediate kick or ban (7-30 days)

Any Offense: Report to High Command

Severe: Permanent ban

Disrespect to Staff

Minor: Formal warning

Moderate: Kick from session

Severe: Ban

Exploiting/Hacking

First Detection: Immediate permanent ban

No warnings given for cheating

3.7 EVIDENCE COLLECTION AND DOCUMENTATION

Proper evidence and documentation are essential for fair moderation.

COLLECTING EVIDENCE:

Screenshots

  • Capture chat logs showing violations
  • Include timestamps when possible
  • Show context, not just isolated messages
  • Ensure text is readable
  • Capture multiple screenshots if needed for full context

Video Recordings

  • Record serious incidents when possible
  • Include audio if relevant
  • Capture enough context before and after
  • Keep recordings for specified retention period
  • Note timestamp and date

Chat Logs

  • Copy relevant conversation sections
  • Include usernames and timestamps
  • Don't edit or alter logs
  • Provide full context
  • Note if logs are partial or incomplete

Witness Statements

  • Document who else observed the incident
  • Get written statements when appropriate
  • Note if witnesses have potential bias
  • Verify witness accounts when possible

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:

Every Moderation Action Must Include:

  • Date and time of incident
  • Username(s) of involved parties
  • Specific rule(s) violated
  • Description of what occurred
  • Evidence collected
  • Action taken
  • Reasoning for chosen action
  • Any relevant context or mitigating factors

Documentation Locations:

  • Case management system (primary)
  • Staff incident log
  • Player's moderation history
  • Any relevant Discord channels

Quality Standards:

  • Be specific and detailed
  • Use objective language
  • Avoid personal opinions
  • Stick to facts
  • Proofread for clarity
  • Complete documentation promptly (within 24 hours)

3.8 PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE SYSTEM

We use a progressive discipline system that escalates consequences for repeat

violations while allowing reform.

DISCIPLINE LEVELS:

Level 1: Verbal Warning

  • Informal reminder of rules
  • Educational approach
  • No formal record (usually)
  • Monitoring for compliance

Level 2: Formal Warning

  • Official warning on record
  • Clear explanation of violation
  • Expectation of immediate improvement
  • Note in moderation history

Level 3: Temporary Removal

  • Kick from session or temporary ban
  • Short cooling-off period
  • Chance to reflect and improve
  • Return with clear expectations

Level 4: Extended Ban

  • Longer temporary ban (days to weeks)
  • Serious violations or patterns
  • Required review before return
  • Conditions for reinstatement

Level 5: Permanent Ban

  • Removal from community
  • Reserved for severe or persistent violations
  • Appeal process available
  • High Command review

PROGRESSION GUIDELINES:

  • Not all violations start at Level 1
  • Severe violations may start at Level 4 or 5
  • Progression considers entire history, not just recent incidents
  • Time between violations may reset progression
  • Good behavior can mitigate past issues
  • Each case evaluated individually within framework

RESET CRITERIA:

Progression may reset if:

  • Significant time has passed without violations (30+ days)
  • Player has shown genuine improvement
  • Previous violations were minor
  • Player has actively contributed positively

Progression does NOT reset for:

  • Severe violations
  • Persistent patterns despite time
  • Multiple different violation types
  • Violations during or shortly after punishments

3.9 APPEALS PROCESS

Players have the right to appeal moderation decisions.

APPEAL ELIGIBILITY:

Players may appeal:

  • Any ban lasting more than 3 days
  • Permanent bans
  • Warnings they believe are unjust
  • Cases with new evidence
  • Actions they feel were biased

Players cannot appeal:

  • Verbal warnings
  • Kicks from sessions (can discuss, but not formal appeal)
  • Decisions already appealed and decided
  • Actions with overwhelming evidence

APPEAL PROCEDURE:

Step 1: Player submits appeal through proper channels

  • Discord ticket system
  • Appeal form if available
  • Must include their side of the story
  • Should provide any evidence supporting their case

Step 2: Staff review

  • Original moderator reviews appeal
  • Higher staff member reviews independently
  • All evidence reconsidered
  • New evidence evaluated
  • Discussion among staff if needed

Step 3: Decision

  • Uphold original decision
  • Reduce punishment
  • Remove punishment entirely
  • Modify conditions
  • Decision explained to player

Step 4: Final determination

  • Most decisions are final after appeal
  • Extreme cases may have additional review
  • New evidence may warrant reopening

APPEAL RESPONSE TIMEFRAME:

  • Acknowledge receipt: 24-48 hours
  • Complete review: 3-7 days
  • Complex cases: Up to 14 days
  • Player kept informed of status

STAFF ROLE IN APPEALS:

When your decision is appealed:

  • Provide all documentation and evidence
  • Explain your reasoning clearly
  • Accept the appeal decision professionally
  • Don't take appeals personally
  • Learn from overturned decisions
  • Don't retaliate against players who appeal

3.11 HANDLING PLAYER REQUESTS FOR STAFF ASSISTANCE

Players have the right to request staff assistance when they witness violations,

experience issues, or need to report concerns. Staff must respond to these

requests promptly and professionally.

!MOD COMMAND IN CHAT:

PURPOSE:

The !mod command is the primary method for players to request immediate staff

assistance during gameplay. When a player types "!mod" in chat, they are

signaling that they need a moderator's attention for:

  • Witnessing a rule violation
  • Being victimized by another player
  • Needing clarification on a situation
  • Reporting suspicious behavior
  • Requesting staff intervention in a dispute

STAFF RESPONSE PROTOCOL:

Step 1: Acknowledge Immediately

  • Respond in chat within 60 seconds if possible
  • Use player's name: "I see your !mod call, [Username]. I'll be with you shortly."
  • If multiple staff are online, first to respond should claim it
  • If you cannot respond immediately, ensure another staff member does

Step 2: Determine Urgency

Ask in chat or DM:

  • "What do you need assistance with?"
  • "Can you briefly describe the situation?"
  • Assess if this requires immediate action or can be scheduled

Step 3: Immediate Response Required For:

  • Active harassment or targeting
  • Ongoing rule violations (VDM, RDM, etc.)
  • Multiple players involved in conflict
  • Safety concerns
  • Situations escalating rapidly

Step 4: Scheduled Response Acceptable For:

  • General questions about rules
  • Past incidents no longer occurring
  • Non-urgent reports
  • Clarification requests
  • Minor concerns

Step 5: Conduct Investigation

  • Use free cam to observe the situation
  • Review chat logs for context
  • Speak with involved parties
  • Gather evidence (screenshots, recordings)
  • Verify facts before taking action

Step 6: Take Appropriate Action

  • Address the violation according to handbook guidelines
  • Conduct mod scene if necessary
  • Issue warnings, kicks, or bans as appropriate
  • Explain outcome to reporting player (privately if sensitive)
  • Thank player for reporting

Step 7: Follow-Up

  • Monitor situation to ensure it's resolved
  • Check on reporting player to confirm they're okay
  • Watch reported player for continued issues
  • Document the entire incident

MULTIPLE !MOD CALLS:

If multiple players call !mod simultaneously:

  • Acknowledge all calls: "I see multiple !mod calls. I'll address each one."
  • Prioritize by urgency and severity
  • Enlist additional staff if available
  • Handle most serious situations first
  • Don't leave anyone without response

If same player calls !mod repeatedly:

  • Respond to first call normally
  • If spamming: "I've acknowledged your call. Please wait while I investigate."
  • If continued spam: "Spamming !mod is against the rules. I'm handling your

report."

  • Take action against spam if excessive (warning/timeout)

VERBAL/VOICE REQUESTS:

If player requests staff in voice chat:

  • Acknowledge verbally: "I heard you. How can I help?"
  • Move to appropriate voice channel if needed
  • Follow same investigation process
  • Document in text after resolution

DIRECT MESSAGES FOR ASSISTANCE:

If player DMs you during session:

  • Respond promptly
  • Determine if issue needs immediate attention
  • Move to appropriate channel for handling
  • Don't handle serious violations entirely in DMs
  • Create proper documentation

PLAYER REQUESTS TO REPORT ANOTHER PLAYER:

DIRECT REPORTS:

When a player says "I need to report [Player]" or similar:

Step 1: Acknowledge and Set Expectations

  • "Thank you for bringing this to my attention."
  • "I'll need to gather some information from you."
  • Explain the process: "I'll speak with you, investigate, and then take

appropriate action."

Step 2: Gather Information Privately

Move to private channel (voice or text) when possible:

  • "Can you explain what happened?"
  • "When did this occur?"
  • "Do you have any evidence (screenshots, etc.)?"
  • "Were there any witnesses?"
  • "How did this affect you or others?"

Step 3: Get Detailed Account

Ask specific questions:

  • "What exactly did they do?"
  • "What rule do you believe was violated?"
  • "Has this happened before with this player?"
  • "Did you attempt to resolve it with them first?" (if appropriate)

Step 4: Evaluate Report Validity

Consider:

  • Does the reported behavior violate rules?
  • Is there supporting evidence?
  • Is the reporter credible?
  • Could this be a misunderstanding?
  • Is there history between these players?
  • Are there any signs of false reporting?

Step 5: Investigate Independently

  • Don't take report at face value without verification
  • Use free cam to observe current behavior
  • Review logs and evidence
  • Check moderation history of both players
  • Speak with other witnesses if available
  • Gather your own evidence

Step 6: Interview Reported Player (If Warranted)

  • Conduct mod scene with reported player
  • "I received a report about [specific behavior]. Can you tell me what happened?"
  • Listen to their side fully
  • Present evidence if appropriate
  • Determine truth of situation

Step 7: Make Decision

Based on investigation:

  • If violation confirmed: Take appropriate disciplinary action
  • If violation not confirmed: No action, or warning if behavior was inappropriate

but not rule-breaking

  • If false report: Address with reporting player
  • If misunderstanding: Educate both parties

Step 8: Communicate Outcome

To Reporting Player:

  • "Thank you for your report. I've investigated and taken appropriate action."
  • Don't share specific punishments (confidential)
  • "Please let me know if this continues or if you have other concerns."
  • If no violation found: "I investigated thoroughly but did not find evidence

of a rule violation. However, I've spoken with all parties involved."

To Reported Player:

  • Explain any consequences given
  • Clarify expectations going forward
  • Answer questions they have
  • Document everything

Step 9: Document Thoroughly

  • Report details from both sides
  • Evidence collected
  • Investigation steps taken
  • Decision and reasoning
  • Actions taken
  • Follow-up needed

PROTECTING REPORTING PLAYERS:

Confidentiality:

  • Don't reveal who reported unless necessary
  • Handle reports discreetly when possible
  • Protect reporters from retaliation
  • Use general observations: "I noticed..." rather than "Someone reported..."

Anti-Retaliation:

  • Watch for retaliation against reporting players
  • Take immediate action against retaliation
  • Reassure reporters they're protected
  • Make clear that retaliation is not tolerated

FALSE REPORTS:

If player makes false or malicious report:

First Instance:

  • Warning about false reporting
  • Explain it wastes staff time and resources
  • Clarify what constitutes a legitimate report
  • Document the false report

Repeated False Reports:

  • Formal warning with documentation
  • Temporary ban from reporting privileges
  • Session removal if severe
  • Potential ban for harassment if targeting someone

Genuine Mistakes:

  • Educate about rules
  • Explain why it wasn't a violation
  • Thank them for trying to help
  • Encourage future reports if uncertain

RESPONSE TIME EXPECTATIONS:

Immediate (Within 1-2 minutes):

  • Active emergencies
  • Ongoing harassment
  • Severe violations in progress
  • Safety concerns

Prompt (Within 5 minutes):

  • Rule violations that just occurred
  • Multiple players involved
  • Escalating situations
  • Urgent concerns

Standard (Within 15 minutes):

  • General reports
  • Past incidents
  • Non-urgent matters
  • Questions and clarifications

IF YOU CANNOT RESPOND:

When unable to handle request:

  • Notify player: "I apologize, I'm currently handling another situation.

[Another staff member] will assist you shortly."

  • Tag another available staff member
  • Brief them on the situation
  • Follow up after to ensure it was handled
  • Document why you couldn't respond

If no staff available:

  • "I'm currently the only staff member online and handling multiple situations.

I will address your concern as soon as possible."

  • Prioritize by severity
  • Request backup if available
  • Document for follow-up

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:

Report About Another Staff Member:

  • Take seriously and remain professional
  • Don't dismiss due to staff status
  • Gather complete information
  • Document thoroughly
  • Escalate to High Command immediately
  • Don't discuss with accused staff member yourself
  • Protect reporting player from potential retaliation
  • Follow Internal Affairs procedures

Report About Yourself:

  • Recuse yourself immediately
  • "I'm involved in this situation, so another staff member will handle it."
  • Request another staff member to investigate
  • Cooperate fully with investigation
  • Don't try to influence outcome
  • Accept decision professionally

Anonymous Reports:

  • Investigate thoroughly even without named reporter
  • Can't follow up directly with reporter
  • Watch for pattern if multiple anonymous reports
  • Take action if violation is confirmed
  • Document that report was anonymous

Reports from Banned Players:

  • Still investigate serious allegations
  • Consider source credibility carefully
  • Don't allow banned player to harass through reports
  • Take action if violation confirmed
  • Document banned player's continued involvement

TEACHING PLAYERS PROPER REPORTING:

Educate Community:

  • Explain when to use !mod
  • Clarify what is reportable
  • Show how to gather evidence
  • Encourage prompt reporting
  • Discourage vigilante justice
  • Explain report process and expectations

Bad Reporting Practices to Discourage:

  • Spamming !mod
  • Reporting for revenge
  • False accusations
  • Reporting instead of simple communication
  • Reporting minor social disagreements
  • Mass reporting to harass someone

Good Reporting Practices to Encourage:

  • Timely reports of actual violations
  • Providing evidence when possible
  • Clear descriptions of issues
  • Respectful communication with staff
  • Patience during investigation
  • Reporting serious concerns promptly

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:

Staff Errors in Handling Reports:

  • Ignoring or dismissing reports without investigation
  • Taking sides before investigating
  • Acting solely on one person's word
  • Revealing reporter identity unnecessarily
  • Failing to follow up with reporter
  • Not documenting properly
  • Showing bias toward friends
  • Over-punishing without evidence
  • Dismissing reports against regular players
  • Rushing to judgment

Best Practices:

  • Respond to every request
  • Investigate thoroughly
  • Remain impartial
  • Document everything
  • Protect all parties appropriately
  • Communicate clearly
  • Follow up on outcomes
  • Learn from each situation

PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS:

Staff members will be evaluated on:

  • Response time to !mod calls
  • Quality of investigations
  • Fair decision-making
  • Professional communication
  • Proper documentation
  • Protection of reporting players
  • Consistency in handling reports

  • Verify correct user
  • Document in notes
  • Inform member of warning

Timeout/Mute:

  • Use appropriate duration
  • Explain reason clearly
  • Monitor for evasion
  • Document the timeout
  • Review for removal if appropriate

Kick:

  • Confirm correct user
  • State clear reason
  • Allow rejoin capability
  • Document incident
  • Monitor if they return

Ban:

  • Verify evidence supports ban
  • Document thoroughly
  • Set appropriate duration
  • Include detailed reason
  • Report to leadership if severe

Role Management:

  • Understand role hierarchy
  • Don't exceed your authority
  • Verify before modifying
  • Document changes
  • Follow role assignment policies

MESSAGE MANAGEMENT:

Deletion:

  • Screenshot before deleting (if evidence needed)
  • Delete inappropriate content immediately
  • Don't delete to hide your mistakes
  • Document what was deleted and why
  • Explain deletions when appropriate

Bulk Operations:

  • Use cautiously
  • Verify scope before executing
  • Have backup/screenshots if needed
  • Announce if clearing large amounts
  • Document mass actions

Pinning:

  • Keep important information pinned
  • Remove outdated pins
  • Don't overcrowd pins
  • Coordinate with other staff
  • Update pins when information changes

PERMISSION MANAGEMENT:

Channel Permissions:

  • Understand permission hierarchy
  • Use temporary overrides appropriately
  • Document permission changes
  • Restore normal permissions when done
  • Don't grant unnecessary access

Role Permissions:

  • Requires higher authority usually
  • Coordinate with administrators
  • Document and justify changes
  • Test before implementing widely
  • Review regularly for appropriateness

5.5 HANDLING DISPUTES AND CONFLICTS

MEMBER DISPUTES:

Assessment:

  • Understand all sides
  • Review relevant history
  • Identify core issues
  • Determine if staff intervention needed
  • Decide appropriate response

Mediation:

  • Remain neutral
  • Listen to both parties
  • Facilitate communication
  • Focus on resolution
  • Document agreement or outcome

Resolution:

  • Separate parties if needed
  • Issue consequences if rules broken
  • Follow up to ensure compliance
  • Monitor future interactions
  • Escalate if unresolved

STAFF-MEMBER CONFLICTS:

If member disputes your action:

  • Stay calm and professional
  • Explain reasoning clearly
  • Listen to their concerns
  • Adjust if you made mistake
  • Stand firm if you're right
  • Escalate to higher staff if needed

If member is hostile:

  • Don't engage in argument
  • Issue timeout if necessary
  • Document interaction
  • Request backup if needed
  • Report harassment to leadership

STAFF-STAFF CONFLICTS:

Prevention:

  • Communicate clearly
  • Respect each other
  • Assume good intentions
  • Discuss disagreements privately
  • Support each other publicly

Resolution:

  • Address issues directly but respectfully
  • Seek compromise
  • Involve leadership if needed
  • Accept decisions gracefully
  • Move forward professionally

5.6 TICKET SYSTEM MANAGEMENT

TICKET RESPONSE:

Initial Response:

  • Acknowledge within 24 hours
  • Introduce yourself
  • Confirm you've read the issue
  • Ask clarifying questions if needed
  • Set expectations for resolution timeline

Investigation:

  • Gather relevant information
  • Review evidence provided
  • Check logs and history
  • Consult other staff if needed
  • Verify facts before deciding

Resolution:

  • Explain decision clearly
  • Provide reasoning
  • Answer follow-up questions
  • Implement solution
  • Document outcome

Follow-up:

  • Check if issue is resolved
  • Address any remaining concerns
  • Thank member for bringing issue forward
  • Close ticket appropriately
  • Note in member's record if relevant

TICKET TYPES:

Ban Appeals:

  • Review original ban details
  • Consider new evidence
  • Evaluate time since ban
  • Assess remorse and understanding
  • Make fair decision
  • Escalate if uncertain

Bug Reports:

  • Document thoroughly
  • Attempt to reproduce
  • Gather technical details
  • Forward to development team
  • Update member on status
  • Close when resolved

Rule Clarifications:

  • Provide accurate information
  • Explain reasoning behind rules
  • Give examples if helpful
  • Suggest reviewing full rules
  • Document if common confusion

Member Reports:

  • Take all reports seriously
  • Investigate thoroughly
  • Protect reporter's confidentiality
  • Take appropriate action
  • Inform reporter of outcome (if appropriate)
  • Follow up as needed

Staff Feedback:

  • Accept feedback gracefully
  • Thank member for input
  • Forward to appropriate leadership
  • Don't take personally
  • Consider for improvement

Partnership Requests:

  • Follow partnership guidelines
  • Evaluate request fairly
  • Consult with leadership
  • Provide clear decision
  • Maintain professional relationships

5.7 VOICE CHANNEL MODERATION

MONITORING VOICE:

Challenges:

  • Can't review history like text
  • Real-time moderation required
  • Harder to document
  • Requires active listening

Strategies:

  • Join channels periodically
  • Respond to reports quickly
  • Move to private channels for discussions
  • Use server mute/deafen if necessary
  • Record serious incidents if policy allows

VOICE VIOLATIONS:

Common Issues:

  • Excessive noise or mic spam
  • Playing music without permission
  • Verbal harassment
  • Inappropriate content/language
  • Talking over others intentionally

Responses:

  • Verbal warning first (usually)
  • Server mute if continuing
  • Move to private channel for discussion
  • Issue formal warnings if needed
  • Ban from voice if severe or repeated

VOICE EVENTS:

Managing Events:

  • Set clear expectations beforehand
  • Use push-to-talk if appropriate
  • Moderate actively during event
  • Address disruptions quickly
  • Facilitate smooth coordination

5.8 CONTENT MODERATION POLICY

PROHIBITED CONTENT:

Never Allowed:

  • Illegal content
  • Child endangerment material (CSAM)
  • Credible threats of violence
  • Doxxing or personal information
  • Malware or phishing
  • Harassment campaigns
  • Hate speech or extremism
  • Non-consensual intimate content

Report Immediately to Platform and Leadership:

  • Illegal content
  • CSAM (also report to NCMEC)
  • Credible threats
  • Severe harassment
  • Platform-wide violations

QUESTIONABLE CONTENT:

Gray Area Handling:

  • Consider context
  • Evaluate intent
  • Check community standards
  • Consult with other staff
  • Document decision and reasoning
  • Be consistent with precedent

When Uncertain:

  • Err on side of caution
  • Remove and review
  • Consult with higher staff
  • Don't make precedent-setting decisions alone

APPEALS OF CONTENT REMOVAL:

If member disputes removal:

  • Review decision
  • Consider their perspective
  • Explain reasoning
  • Adjust if you were wrong
  • Uphold if correct
  • Escalate if complicated

6. REPORTING AND DOCUMENTATION

6.1 INCIDENT REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

WHAT TO REPORT:

Always Report:

  • Any ban issued
  • Serious rule violations
  • Harassment or targeted behavior
  • Threats of violence or self-harm
  • Potential illegal activity
  • Staff misconduct
  • Security breaches
  • Coordinated attacks or raids
  • Appeals of major decisions

Report When Appropriate:

  • Patterns of concerning behavior
  • Recurring issues with individuals
  • Technical problems affecting community
  • Policy clarification needs
  • Suggestions for improvements

REPORTING METHODS:

Formal Reports:

  • Staff incident reporting system
  • Leadership tickets
  • Email to appropriate leadership
  • Documented case notes

Informal Communication:

  • Staff chat mentions
  • Direct messages to supervisors
  • Team meetings or debriefs
  • Verbal reports (follow up in writing)

6.2 DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS

QUALITY DOCUMENTATION:

Essential Elements:

  • Date and time (with timezone)
  • All involved parties
  • Detailed description of events
  • Evidence collected
  • Actions taken
  • Reasoning for decisions
  • Relevant context
  • Follow-up needed

Writing Style:

  • Clear and concise
  • Objective and factual
  • Specific details
  • Chronological order
  • Professional language
  • Proofread for accuracy

EVIDENCE PRESERVATION:

Types of Evidence:

  • Screenshots
  • Video recordings
  • Chat logs
  • Witness statements
  • System logs
  • Voice recordings (if permitted)

Preservation Requirements:

  • Save original files
  • Don't edit or alter
  • Include timestamps
  • Organize clearly
  • Secure sensitive evidence
  • Maintain chain of custody

CASE NOTES:

Purpose:

  • Provide context for future staff
  • Document decision-making process
  • Support appeals review
  • Track patterns over time
  • Ensure accountability

What to Include:

  • Summary of incident
  • Relevant history
  • Evidence evaluated
  • Decision-making factors
  • Action taken and why
  • Any unusual circumstances
  • Follow-up required

6.3 RECORD RETENTION

RETENTION PERIODS:

Warnings:

  • Retain for minimum 1 year
  • May be kept longer for serious violations
  • Consider for removal if excellent behavior

Bans:

  • Permanent record of all bans
  • Include all relevant documentation
  • Never delete even if ban is lifted

Appeals:

  • Keep all appeal documentation
  • Link to original case
  • Document decision process
  • Retain indefinitely

Logs:

  • Chat logs: Per platform policy and legal requirements
  • Moderation logs: Indefinitely
  • System logs: As needed for functionality
  • Evidence: Until case is fully resolved plus retention period

PRIVACY CONSIDERATIONS:

  • Protect personal information
  • Share only with authorized staff
  • Secure storage of records
  • Follow data protection laws
  • Purge records on request if legally required
  • Anonymize when possible for training

6.4 INTERNAL AFFAIRS INVESTIGATIONS

PURPOSE:

Internal Affairs investigates:

  • Staff misconduct allegations
  • Abuse of power
  • Policy violations by staff
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Breaches of confidentiality
  • Bias or unfair treatment claims

PROCESS:

  1. Report received
  2. Initial evaluation for validity
  3. Formal investigation opened if warranted
  4. Evidence gathered
  5. Interviews conducted
  6. Findings documented
  7. Decision made
  8. Action taken if necessary
  9. Follow-up and monitoring

STAFF COOPERATION:

If you're involved in IA investigation:

  • Cooperate fully and honestly
  • Provide all requested information
  • Don't interfere with investigation
  • Don't retaliate against reporters
  • Maintain confidentiality
  • Accept outcomes professionally

OUTCOMES:

Possible Results:

  • No action (unfounded)
  • Coaching or retraining
  • Formal warning
  • Demotion
  • Suspension
  • Removal from staff
  • Community ban (severe cases)

6.5 TRANSPARENCY AND ACCOUNTABILITY

PUBLIC ACCOUNTABILITY:

Community Transparency:

  • Announce major bans when appropriate
  • Explain policy changes
  • Share statistics periodically
  • Accept feedback gracefully
  • Acknowledge and correct mistakes
  • Maintain trust through openness

Limitations:

  • Don't share private details
  • Protect victim privacy
  • Maintain investigation confidentiality
  • Don't discuss ongoing cases publicly
  • Balance transparency with discretion

STAFF ACCOUNTABILITY:

Internal Accountability:

  • Accept responsibility for errors
  • Learn from mistakes
  • Support fair review processes
  • Hold each other accountable respectfully
  • Document decision-making
  • Welcome constructive feedback

Checks and Balances:

  • Higher staff review major decisions
  • Appeal processes exist
  • Multiple staff perspectives on complex issues
  • Regular performance reviews
  • Community feedback mechanisms

9. STAFF CONDUCT VIOLATIONS AND CONSEQUENCES

9.1 MINOR INFRACTIONS

EXAMPLES:

  • Occasional inactivity without notice
  • Minor attitude issues
  • Small mistakes in judgment
  • Isolated unprofessional behavior
  • Minor policy oversights
  • Communication lapses

CONSEQUENCES:

  • Verbal coaching
  • Reminder of expectations
  • Additional training
  • Increased oversight
  • Documentation in file

9.2 MAJOR INFRACTIONS

EXAMPLES:

  • Abuse of staff powers
  • Consistent bias or favoritism
  • Serious policy violations
  • Inappropriate conduct
  • Failure to perform duties
  • Conflicts of interest violations
  • Multiple minor infractions

CONSEQUENCES:

  • Formal warning
  • Temporary suspension
  • Demotion
  • Required retraining
  • Probationary period
  • Removal from specific duties

9.3 TERMINATION GROUNDS

IMMEDIATE REMOVAL OFFENSES:

  • Harassment or bullying
  • Discrimination or hate speech
  • Sharing confidential information
  • Serious abuse of power
  • Illegal activities
  • Intentional sabotage
  • Gross misconduct
  • Refusing to cooperate with IA

PROCEDURE:

  1. Investigation conducted
  2. Staff member notified
  3. Opportunity to respond
  4. Decision made by leadership
  5. Removal executed
  6. Permissions revoked
  7. Documentation completed
  8. Community notification if appropriate

9.4 APPEAL PROCESS FOR STAFF

STAFF MEMBER RIGHTS:

  • Understand accusations
  • Present your side
  • Provide evidence
  • Request review
  • Appeal decisions
  • Fair treatment

APPEAL PROCESS:

  1. Submit appeal to leadership
  2. Case reviewed independently
  3. Additional information gathered
  4. Decision reconsidered
  5. Final determination made
  6. Outcome communicated
